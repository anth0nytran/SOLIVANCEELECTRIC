### Phase 1: Technical Audit
When given a site URL or crawl data:
- Analyze crawl results (response codes, titles, metas, internal linking, indexing signals, canonicals, redirects, page speed, mobile-friendliness, etc.).
- Prioritize issues by impact on SEO visibility, AEO extraction, and GEO citability (e.g., structured data opportunities, thin content, slow load times that affect AI crawling).
- Categorize: Critical, High, Medium, Low with estimated impact on all three engines.
- Recommend fixes including schema markup opportunities (FAQPage, HowTo, Article, etc.) that boost AEO/GEO performance.
- Suggest plain-English commands for deeper analysis.

### Phase 2: Keyword & Intent Research (SEO + AEO + GEO)
When given seed topics, competitors, or a niche:
- Research traditional keywords (volume, competition, CPC) plus AEO/GEO-specific opportunities: conversational questions, "how/why/what" queries, People Also Ask patterns, and AI-style prompts.
- Cluster by user intent and funnel stage, identifying topics with high citation potential in generative answers.
- Highlight long-tail question-based opportunities and content gaps where we can own unique angles or data.
- Deliver a unified map: primary targets, supporting terms, AEO question clusters, and GEO-optimized content pillar/cluster structure with estimated opportunity across engines.

### Phase 3: Content Outlining & Multi-Engine SERP/Gap Analysis
For any target topic or keyword:
1. Analyze top results across traditional SERP + AI answer engines (focus on what ranks in Google and what appears in AI summaries).
2. Map common structures, formats, and gaps.
3. Perform gap analysis for SEO (rankings), AEO (direct extractable answers), and GEO (citation likelihood).
4. Produce a detailed outline that includes:
   - Recommended title, meta description, and URL slug
   - Answer-first structure: concise direct answer in the first 150 words (AEO/GEO optimized)
   - Full H1–H6 hierarchy with section targets
   - Built-in opportunities for statistics, expert quotes, citations, comparisons, tables, and lists
   - Schema recommendations (FAQ, HowTo, etc.)
   - Internal linking targets and entity signals
   - Tone guidelines that balance authoritative voice with conversational fluency

### Phase 4: Content Creation (Multi-Engine Optimized)
When given an outline, target topic, and brand voice references:
- Write reader-first content that is simultaneously SEO-ranked, AEO-answerable, and GEO-citable.
- Use proven structures: answer-first (TL;DR or direct response upfront), then depth with scannable sections, bullets, tables, and natural language.
- Incorporate GEO techniques: authoritative tone, clear statistics, inline citations, expert quotes, domain-specific insights, and unique angles.
- Adapt to effective formats (how-to, comparison, listicle, pillar guide, FAQ-heavy, etc.) while ensuring natural flow.
- Optimize for scannability and AI parsing: short paragraphs, descriptive headings, semantic structure, and proper handling of facts/sources.
- Include natural internal/external linking and E-E-A-T signals.

### Phase 5: Multi-Engine Quality Review (Expanded 9-Pass Editing)
For any draft, run these distinct passes and output a changelog + revised version:
1. Accuracy & logic — fix any impractical or incorrect information.
2. Fact-checking — flag or replace unsourced/hallucinated claims with citations.
3. Voice & readability — ensure human, fluent, conversational yet authoritative tone (GEO boost).
4. Audience alignment — confirm depth and style match the target reader.
5. Specificity & value — replace vague content with actionable, unique insights.
6. SEO elements — check technical signals, keywords, and internal links.
7. AEO readiness — verify direct answers early, FAQ structure, and extractability.
8. GEO citability — confirm statistics, quotes, citations, scannability, and authority signals.
9. Competitive edge — ensure we meet or exceed top results across traditional SERP and AI outputs.

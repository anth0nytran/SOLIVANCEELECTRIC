01 — Executive Summary

The market is winnable — and the winners all leak.

We scored the top five Houston commercial electrical contractors across eight criteria. Only one cracked 67 / 80. The patterns separating the winners from the losers are consistent, teachable, and fixable in a single build cycle.

The category leaders are Beekman (67) and FSG (66) — one runs a local-authority playbook (370+ Google reviews, named B2B clients), the other runs an enterprise-credibility playbook (NRG Stadium, GEICO, displayed license numbers). Both win through proof, not design.

The middle of the pack — Colwell, Wired, RMG — is technically competent but visually dated, inconsistently branded, and weak on conversion surface area. The ceiling to beat is lower than it looks.

Solivance's current codebase already out-designs every competitor in this study — Bricolage Grotesque display type, industrial squared-off radii, Framer Motion choreography, and a navy + orange palette that matches the category conventions without being derivative. The visual chassis is class-leading.

The gap is proof, not polish. Solivance ships today with zero testimonials, no customer logos, no license number in-hero, no certification stack, and placeholder imagery across every hero slot. Close those five gaps and this site outperforms every competitor on the list — not because it's prettier, but because it's credible.

04 — Patterns of the Top 10%

Five moves that separate winners from the rest.

These are the patterns Beekman, FSG, Colwell, and Wired execute consistently — and RMG doesn't. Execute four of five and Solivance lands in the top decile at launch.

01

Longevity and license numbers live in the hero strip.

FSG opens with "Licensed, Insured, and Experienced Since 1982 · License #17904." Beekman anchors with "Over 40 years." Commercial buyers comparing three quotes weight this signal heavily — they want proof the contractor will still exist next year and passes inspection on the first pass. Solivance's config lists "10 years" but buries it. Fix: surface it in the hero trust band alongside the Texas TECL number.
02

Named B2B customer logos outperform generic testimonials.

FSG's NRG Stadium / GEICO / Houston Botanical Garden / Ronald McDonald House row and Beekman's HEB / Hobby Lobby / 5 Below strip are the highest-converting modules on either site. Commercial buyers don't trust stars — they trust brands they recognize. Solivance ships zero logos today. This is the single highest-leverage gap to close before launch.
03

Review-count proof or certification-stack proof. Never neither.

Beekman went reviews-first (4.9 · 370+). Colwell went cert-first (BBB + IEC + NFPA + ABC + IAEI). Both work. Pre-launch Solivance has zero reviews, so the cert route is the answer — stack 3–5 real affiliations (BBB, IEC, NFPA, Texas TECL, insurance carrier) in a horizontal trust band until review velocity catches up.
04

A memorable one-liner beats an SEO-string headline.

Colwell's "You call, we show up, and the job gets done" and FSG's "Done Right Guarantee" out-perform Beekman's keyword-stuffed H1. Solivance's current hero — "Commercial electrical, done right." — is already best-in-class on this axis. Hold the line. Don't regress toward an SEO string.
05

Real on-site photography beats placeholders every time.

FSG, Beekman, Wired, and RMG all run genuine on-site photography — crews at panels, generators, parking lots. Solivance is the only site in the comparison running /placeholder.svg in every hero slot. Five real photos (hero, panel, generator, parking-lot pole, crew portrait) close the visual deficit. Commercial stock from Unsplash / a paid library is an acceptable bridge until a shoot is scheduled — raw placeholders are not.
05 — SEO Landscape

Where Solivance can win cleanly.

Five keyword opportunities with either weak competition or unclaimed territory — mapped to content Solivance already has in its config.

Opportunity · 01"Pedestal installation Houston"

FSG and RMG don't own this term. Competition is minimal. Solivance's serviceData already has strong pedestal copy — ship it as a dedicated service page with FAQ and geo tags and it can rank within a quarter.

Opportunity · 02"Mobile home electrical [suburb]"

Near-zero commercial competition on this query family. Solivance has dedicated service copy and a real audience in Houston's mobile home corridors. Low effort, high yield.

Opportunity · 03Suburb + "commercial EV charger"

Beekman and Colwell own the parent EV term. But "commercial EV charger Sugar Land / Katy / Richmond" is underserved. Build five geo-targeted landing pages off the existing EV service copy.

Opportunity · 04ZIP-targeted queries

siteConfig.zipCodes lists 77048, 77407, 77478, 77493, 77471. "77048 commercial electrician" and siblings face zero serious competition. Five ZIP-level pages with unique intro copy unlock near-free traffic.

Three to five Spanish service pages (panel upgrades, generator installs, EV chargers) land Solivance in an entirely uncontested market segment and double the addressable buyer pool in Greater Houston. Estimated lift: the largest single SEO win on the list.

06 — Recommended Direction

Keep the chassis, add the proof.

Solivance's current codebase already beats four of five competitors on visual design alone. The direction is refinement, not rebuild.

Palette

Hold the navy + orange. Rename the legacy --onestop-red and --onestop-gold to --brand-navy, --brand-ink, --brand-accent — the current token aliasing is a bug waiting to bite. Consider a warm off-white surface (#f6f4f0) for mid-page sections to break up the white-block monotony every competitor has.

Typography

Bricolage Grotesque for display, Geist for body, JetBrains Mono for eyebrows — keep exactly as shipped. None of the five competitors runs a distinct display face. This alone puts Solivance visually one tier above the category. Hold the line.

Trust architecture

Insert a dedicated trust row immediately below the hero: Licensed · Insured · Since 2015 · TECL #[number] · BBB · IEC. Replace the current placeholder testimonial empty-state with a certification stack (5 logos) until reviews accrue. Shippable in hours.

Social proof

Even before the first client logo arrives, a "Recent Projects" grid with 4–6 anonymized scope cards (e.g., "200A panel upgrade · Sugar Land office · 2 days") out-converts placeholder blocks by an order of magnitude. Quote anonymized when necessary.

Photography

Blocking issue. Commission five photos before launch — hero crew shot, panel close-up, generator install, parking-lot pole, EV charger. Until shoot day, licensed commercial stock is acceptable; /placeholder.svg is not.

Motion

Keep the Framer Motion entrance choreography — it already feels editorial. Add one signature: a scroll-triggered counter on the "Since 2015 · [n] projects · [n] inspections passed" band, rendered in Bricolage Grotesque numerals. Matches RMG's stats-band pattern, executed with better type.

What to avoid

  • SEO-string headlines (Beekman's failure mode — geo terms stuffed into H1)
  • Company-name H1s (RMG's failure mode — zero positioning)
  • Single stock hero photo reliance (Wired's weakness — visually unmemorable)
  • Phone number only in the header (every competitor repeats it 3–5× for a reason)
  • Shipping any page with /placeholder.svg user-facing
  • Over-rounding radii (the current squared-off industrial feel outperforms the soft Beekman/Colwell look)

## Patterns of the Top 10%

These are the five moves that separate 60+ scores from the rest:

### 1. **Longevity + license numbers in the hero or trust band**
FSG: "Licensed, Insured, and Experienced Since 1982 · License #17904." Beekman: "Over 40 years." RMG: "HMEL #722." When a commercial buyer compares quotes, they want proof the contractor will still exist next year and passes inspection. **Solivance says "10 years" once — it's buried.**

### 2. **Named B2B customer logos or case studies**
FSG (NRG Stadium, GEICO, Houston Botanical Garden) and Beekman (HEB, Hobby Lobby) both weaponize this. Commercial buyers don't trust generic testimonials; they trust logos they recognize. **Solivance has zero logos shipped today — highest-leverage gap.**

### 3. **Review-count proof or certification-stack proof**
Beekman chose "4.9 / 370+ Google reviews." Colwell chose "BBB + IEC + NFPA + ABC + IAEI." Both work. **Solivance has neither — needs the cert route until reviews accumulate.**

### 4. **Memorable one-liner, not an SEO-string headline**
Colwell's *"You call, we show up, and the job gets done"* and FSG's *"Done Right Guarantee"* both out-perform Beekman's keyword-stuffed H1. **Solivance's current hero — "Commercial electrical, done right." — is already best-in-class on this axis. Don't regress to SEO strings.**

### 5. **Real on-site photography beats placeholders, every time**
FSG, Beekman, Wired, RMG all feature actual electricians working on real panels, lots, generators. **Solivance is the only site in the comparison running `/placeholder.svg` in every hero — the single most expensive visual gap to close before launch.**

## SEO Landscape

### Keyword space Solivance is targeting
Current `keywords` array in `layout.tsx` covers:
- `commercial electrical contractor Houston TX` (high intent, high competition)
- `panel upgrades Houston TX`
- `generator installation Houston TX`
- `parking lot lighting Houston TX`
- `EV charger installation Houston TX`
- `commercial electrician [ZIP 77048]`
- Plus suburb variants (Sugar Land, Richmond, Katy, Pearland, etc.)

### Where competitors rank
Beekman, Colwell, FSG all rank page 1 for `commercial electrician Houston`. RMG ranks on industrial-specific terms. Wired owns generator-adjacent queries.

### Opportunity gaps
1. **"Pedestal installation Houston"** — RMG/FSG don't own it; minimal competition. Solivance can win this cleanly.
2. **"Mobile home electrical connection Houston / Sugar Land"** — nearly no commercial site competes here.
3. **"Commercial EV charger installation [suburb]"** — Beekman and Colwell own the parent keyword, but suburb + "commercial EV" is underserved.
4. **"77048 commercial electrician"** — ZIP-specific queries (currently in keywords) face zero serious competition.
5. **Spanish-language content** — Solivance's config lists `knowsLanguage: ["en","es"]` but no Spanish pages exist. Beekman and Wired don't have this either. Writing 3–5 Spanish-language service pages is an outsized SEO + trust win in Houston's Latino contractor buyer market.

### SEO content-depth recommendation
Competitors publish 200–500 words per service page. Solivance's service data already has strong `longDescription` copy — ensure each service renders a full anchor page with:
- H1 = service name + geo
- 300+ words of unique copy (already have it in `serviceData`)
- Service-area list below fold
- FAQ block (reuse from siteConfig)
- Schema.org `Service` markup (layout already has `OfferCatalog` — extend per-page)

## Recommended Design Direction (backed by data)

### Color
- **Keep navy + orange**, but **tighten the palette**: rename `--onestop-red` and `--onestop-gold` to distinct roles — `--brand-navy` + `--brand-accent` (retire the `onestop-` prefix left over from the scaffold). Consider a **second accent** (deep charcoal or a warm steel) for section dividers to avoid the "navy block → orange button" pattern everyone in the category is using.
- **Differentiation move:** Solivance's codebase palette (`#0f2847` + `#F26B1F`) is stronger than Beekman's pale blue and more modern than RMG's corporate blue. Double down.

### Typography
- Bricolage Grotesque (display) + Geist (body) + JetBrains Mono (eyebrow labels) — **keep exactly as is**. None of the five competitors use a distinct display face; this alone elevates Solivance's visual tier.

### Structure refinements
- Add a **"Since 2015" / "10 years" + license number** band to the hero or top trust strip (matches FSG/Beekman/Colwell). Even 10 years is credible if stated confidently.
- Replace empty testimonial slot with a **certification stack row**: BBB (if member), IEC, NFPA, TECL #, insurance carrier. Quickest win while reviews accumulate.
- Add a **"Recent Projects"** section with 4–6 project cards — even 2–3 blurred/anonymized ones beat zero. Commercial buyers want proof of scope.
- Keep the industrial square-radius aesthetic — it already outperforms the rounded Beekman/Colwell look.

### Photography
- **Blocking issue before launch:** real hero imagery (panel, generator, parking lot pole, EV charger, warehouse interior, crew portrait). Five photos cover the whole site. Until shot, stock photography from a commercial source is acceptable — placeholders are not.

### Animation
- Framer Motion entrance is tasteful. **Keep.** Add one signature: a scroll-triggered counter on the "Since 2015 · [n] projects" band (matches RMG's "1000+ Projects" pattern but executed with Bricolage Grotesque numerals, not default).

### What to AVOID (competitor failure modes)
- Don't stuff the H1 with geo terms (Beekman's failure mode).
- Don't regress to a generic "Company Name" H1 (RMG's failure mode).
- Don't over-rely on a single stock hero photo (Wired's weakness).
- Don't hide the phone number in the header only — repeat it in hero, mid-page, footer (everyone does this for a reason).
- Don't ship with `/placeholder.svg` anywhere user-facing.
